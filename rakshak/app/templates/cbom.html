Each row in the CBOM detail tabs is colour-coded by its quantum safety tier. The Safety badge shows the classification at a glance.

BadgeWhat it meansExamples
✓ PQC Safe NIST-standardised PQC algorithm. Resistant to Shor's and Grover's algorithms. ML-DSA-44/65/87, ML-KEM-768, Falcon-512, SLH-DSA
✓ Safe Classical algorithm safe against Grover's (256-bit key or strong hash). Not vulnerable to Shor's. AES-256-GCM, ChaCha20-Poly1305, SHA-256, SHA-384, TLS 1.3
⚠ Marginal 128-bit symmetric — halved to ~64 bits by Grover's algorithm. Borderline acceptable; upgrade recommended. AES-128-GCM, AES-128-CBC, Camellia-128, ARIA-128, TLS 1.2
⚠ Classical Vulnerable to Shor's algorithm on a cryptanalytically relevant quantum computer (CRQC). Must be replaced. RSA, ECDSA, ECDHE, X25519, DHE, Ed25519
✗ Broken Broken by classical cryptanalysis, not just quantum. Immediate remediation required. SHA-1, MD5, 3DES, RC4, DES, SSLv2/v3, TLS 1.0/1.1
Classification is based on NIST FIPS 203/204/205 and CISA PQC Migration Guidance. The Safety column is auto-detected from the algorithm name — open the Guide page for the full reference table.
