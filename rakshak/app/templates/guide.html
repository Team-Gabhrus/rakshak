PQC Posture Definitions

Rakshak dynamically classifies your interconnected assets into four definitive tiers. These tiers strictly adhere to our established criteria outlining the migration from classical Shor's-vulnerable algorithms to full NIST Post-Quantum Cryptography compliance.

Assigned Label Classification Criteria Real-World Example
❌ Not Quantum-Safe HNDL Vulnerable. The system relies entirely on classical public-key cryptography (like ECDHE, RSA) that is fundamentally broken by a future Quantum Computer running Shor's algorithm. Classical key exchange (ECDHE or RSA) combined with any symmetric cipher (even AES-256).
🟡 Partially Quantum-Safe HNDL Protected. The system deploys PQC protections in Key Exchange to neutralize the "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" threat, but still relies on classical cryptography for Authentication. Hybrid PQC Key Exchange (X25519+ML-KEM-768) combined with an RSA-2048 identity certificate.
🔵 PQC-Ready Transitional Infrastructure. The underlying architecture supports PQC algorithms across critical layers (e.g. PQC KEX + PQC Authentication) but may still rely on legacy Root CAs. ML-KEM-768 Key Exchange bonded with an ML-DSA-65 certificate, signed by a legacy RSA Root CA.
🟢 Fully Quantum-Safe End-to-End Compliant. All cryptographic operations across every layer (including the entire Certificate Trust Chain and internal APIs) enforce NIST standardized PQC algorithms natively. ML-KEM-768 Key Exchange over AES-256-GCM, fully authenticated via an unbroken ML-DSA / SLH-DSA trust chain.

Platform Feature Guide (Hackathon Demo Script)

Follow these steps to demonstrate every core capability of the Rakshak platform to the judges:

Go to the Home or Asset Inventory page and scan these specific targets to prove the Deep Packet Inspection and grading matrix works perfectly:

  • 🟢 Fully Quantum Safe: Scan test.openquantumsafe.org (Demonstrates ML-KEM + ML-DSA integration).
  • 🔵 PQC-Ready: Assessed via internal audit integration (e.g., confirming library/infrastructure agility).
  • 🟡 Partially Quantum-Safe: Scan pq.cloudflareresearch.com (Demonstrates ML-KEM hybrid key exchange blocking HNDL attacks).
  • ❌ Not Quantum-Safe: Scan cloudflare.com or demo-qs.pnb.in (Detects purely classical key exchange exposing the session to Harvest Now, Decrypt Later).
  • ⚪ Unknown: Scan 192.0.2.1 (Demonstrates graceful failure for unreachable endpoints).

  • Selective Re-Scanning: Navigate to Asset Inventory, check the boxes next to multiple rows, and click ▶️ Scan Selected to trigger a bulk background scan.
  • URL Normalization: Add https://pnbindia.in/ (with a slash) and https://pnbindia.in to demonstrate that Rakshak is smart enough to normalize them into a single asset.
  • Asset Discovery: Go to Asset Discovery and click "Trigger Discovery". Rakshak will recursively enumerate DNS, IPs, Subnets, and Server Software for all known assets and push them into the triage queues.

After scanning a target, navigate to the CBOM dashboard:

  • Click "View JSON" on any asset to reveal the deeply nested Cryptographic Bill of Materials.
  • Point out how Rakshak successfully extracted the X.509 Certificate chain, the exact symmetric cipher block, and whether the underlying Object Identifiers (OIDs) belong to NIST PQC standards.

  • Enterprise Rating: Go to Cyber Rating to show how the organizational risk score (1-1000) dynamically fluctuates based on the ratio of PQC-ready assets versus vulnerable legacy endpoints.
  • Actionable Remediation: Go to Posture of PQC or Reporting to show the Playbooks. For every 🔴 Not Quantum-Safe asset, Rakshak dynamically generates a step-by-step recommendation (e.g., "Upgrade encryption from AES-128 to AES-256-GCM").

  • Concurrent Sessions: Open Rakshak in an Incognito window and log in as admin. Your original session will instantly flag "Session expired or superseded" on its next action, proving concurrent logging is strictly prohibited.
  • Audit Logs: Go to Users and check the Audit Logs table. Point out the "Details" column which seamlessly captures the device Browser mapping and Geographic Location via reverse IP lookup.
  • Hash Verification: Hover over any 16-character Audit Log Hash to see the full SHA-256 cryptographic proof. Click the hash to instantly copy it to your clipboard for forensic verification.
