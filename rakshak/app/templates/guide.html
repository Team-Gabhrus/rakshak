PQC Posture Definitions

Rakshak dynamically classifies your interconnected assets into four definitive tiers. These tiers strictly adhere to our established criteria outlining the migration from classical Shor's-vulnerable algorithms to full NIST Post-Quantum Cryptography compliance.

Assigned Label Classification Criteria Real-World Example
❌ Not Quantum-Safe No PQC Detected. No Post-Quantum Cryptography is detected anywhere — neither in the negotiated TLS cipher suite (key exchange) nor in the server's X.509 certificate chain (signature algorithm OIDs). The connection relies entirely on classical cryptography vulnerable to Shor's algorithm. ECDHE key exchange + RSA/ECDSA certificate + AES-256-GCM. Scan targets: cloudflare.com, google.com.
🟡 Partially Quantum-Safe PQC Detected in One Layer. At least one PQC algorithm has been detected — either in the negotiated cipher suite (ML-KEM key exchange) or from the server's leaf certificate signature OID (ML-DSA, SLH-DSA). However, full PQC migration across the certificate trust chain is not yet complete. Server presenting an ML-DSA leaf certificate but with a classical RSA Intermediate CA. Or: ML-KEM key exchange with a classical RSA identity cert.
🔵 PQC-Ready PQC in KEX + Auth. Both the key exchange and the leaf certificate's authentication use PQC algorithms (detected via real X.509 OID parsing). However, at least one Intermediate or Root CA in the trust chain still uses a classical signature, preventing end-to-end quantum resilience. ML-DSA-65 leaf certificate (OID: 2.16.840.1.101.3.4.3.18) + PQC key exchange, but a Root CA signed with RSA-2048. Scan target: test.openquantumsafe.org (if Root CA is classical).
🟢 Fully Quantum-Safe End-to-End PQC. Every certificate in the trust chain (Leaf → Intermediate → Root CA) uses a NIST-standardized PQC signature OID (ML-DSA, SLH-DSA, or FN-DSA). No classical asymmetric cryptography remains in any layer of the connection. ML-DSA-65 leaf + ML-DSA-87 Intermediate + ML-DSA-87 Root CA — entire chain verified via OID inspection. Scan target: test.openquantumsafe.org (if full chain is PQC).

How It Works — Scanning Architecture

Rakshak uses a dual-engine scanning architecture to detect PQC usage in real-time — no hardcoded mocks or fake data:

#EngineWhat It DoesDetects
1 sslyze (Primary) Performs a standard TLS handshake via the system's OpenSSL. Enumerates all supported cipher suites, TLS versions, and downloads the X.509 certificate chain. Classical ciphers (ECDHE, RSA, AES), cert chain OIDs, TLS versions
2 OQS Docker Probe 🐳 Runs openssl s_client -brief inside the openquantumsafe/curl Docker container, which ships with liboqs-enabled OpenSSL capable of negotiating PQC cipher suites and parsing PQC certificates. ML-DSA, Falcon, SLH-DSA signatures; ML-KEM key exchange; PQC cert chain depth
── Scan Pipeline ──────────────────────────────────────────
🔍 Step 1: User enters target (e.g., test.openquantumsafe.org:6182)
🔒 Step 2: sslyze attempts TLS handshake via system OpenSSL
🐳 Step 3: OQS Docker probe runs in parallel — detects PQC signatures
🏷️ Step 4: Best result (classical or PQC) is selected → pqc_classifier.classify()
📊 Step 5: Classification walks the cert chain OIDs → assigns label
Why not just sslyze?
Standard OpenSSL cannot negotiate PQC cipher suites or parse PQC certificates. The TLS handshake fails entirely on PQC-only servers.
Why OQS Docker?
The OQS container bundles liboqs + oqs-provider, enabling our scanner to speak PQC natively and detect ML-DSA, Falcon, ML-KEM algorithms from real connections.
Dual-stack servers?
Servers supporting both classical + PQC send classical certs to classical clients. The OQS probe discovers their hidden PQC capability and upgrades the classification.

Platform Feature Guide (Hackathon Demo Script)

Follow these steps to demonstrate every core capability of the Rakshak platform to the judges:

Go to the Home or Asset Inventory page and scan these specific targets to prove the Deep Packet Inspection and grading matrix works perfectly:

  • 🟢 Fully Quantum Safe: Scan test.openquantumsafe.org:6182 — OQS Docker probe detects ML-DSA-44 signature and PQC intermediate CA. Also try :6095 (Falcon-512).
  • ❌ Not Quantum-Safe: Scan cloudflare.com or google.com — Classical RSA/ECDSA certs. OQS probe confirms no PQC signatures anywhere.
  • ⚪ Unknown: Scan 192.0.2.1 — Demonstrates graceful failure for unreachable endpoints.

  • Selective Re-Scanning: Navigate to Asset Inventory, check the boxes next to multiple rows, and click ▶️ Scan Selected to trigger a bulk background scan.
  • URL Normalization: Add https://pnbindia.in/ (with a slash) and https://pnbindia.in to demonstrate that Rakshak is smart enough to normalize them into a single asset.
  • Asset Discovery: Go to Asset Discovery and click "Trigger Discovery". Rakshak will recursively enumerate DNS, IPs, Subnets, and Server Software for all known assets and push them into the triage queues.

After scanning a target, navigate to the CBOM dashboard:

  • Click "View JSON" on any asset to reveal the deeply nested Cryptographic Bill of Materials.
  • Point out how Rakshak successfully extracted the X.509 Certificate chain, the exact symmetric cipher block, and whether the underlying Object Identifiers (OIDs) belong to NIST PQC standards.

  • Enterprise Rating: Go to Cyber Rating to show how the organizational risk score (1-1000) dynamically fluctuates based on the ratio of PQC-ready assets versus vulnerable legacy endpoints.
  • Actionable Remediation: Go to Posture of PQC or Reporting to show the Playbooks. For every 🔴 Not Quantum-Safe asset, Rakshak dynamically generates a step-by-step recommendation (e.g., "Upgrade encryption from AES-128 to AES-256-GCM").

  • Concurrent Sessions: Open Rakshak in an Incognito window and log in as admin. Your original session will instantly flag "Session expired or superseded" on its next action, proving concurrent logging is strictly prohibited.
  • Audit Logs: Go to Users and check the Audit Logs table. Point out the "Details" column which seamlessly captures the device Browser mapping and Geographic Location via reverse IP lookup.
  • Hash Verification: Hover over any 16-character Audit Log Hash to see the full SHA-256 cryptographic proof. Click the hash to instantly copy it to your clipboard for forensic verification.
